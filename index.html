Synopsis

'The Four Treasures' is a 2D Roguelite game created in Unity. It is set in a mythical rendition of Celtic Ireland. Many of the characters in the game take inspiration from those of famous Irish myths. 'The Four Treasures' centres around Tarann, a member of the Tuatha Dé Danann, a supernatural clan who ruled over Ireland before disappearing. To save their clan from the wrath of Balor of the Evil Eye, Tarann sets out on a quest to retrieve the four treasures of the Tuatha. The treasures are now scattered over Ireland and are being protected by the Morrigan, the Banshee, Fionn Mac Cumhaill and Balor. Will Tarann succeed in retrieving the four treasures and save their clan?


Project Description

'The Four Treasures' features four procedurally generated levels ensuring the player has a unique experience every game. The layout of each level is created using a dungeon generator which is made up of equal sized rooms with different placed entrances. Rooms are connected based on entrance locations. The levels background is also procedurally generated using Perlin noise. Both of these are seeded using the title the player chose to name their run. This name is hashed with the one of the following suffixes (_East, _North, _West, _South) ensuing each of the levels generated has a unique layout. Hidden rooms, collectables and enemies are randomly placed in the levels, encouraging players to explore the world. Having levels be procedurally generated allows for replay ability whilst saving development time in not having to manually create multiple level variations.

Adaptive AI is featured in 'The Four Treasures' in the form of Unity Behaviour Graphs. The final boss battle with Balor utilizes the behaviour graphs abilities. Using a custom node the AI is able to change its decisions based on statistics collected from the player. This custom node chooses the enemy's next move using weighted random decisions which change in accordance to the player's actions during the battle. If players are using a majority of long range attacks the enemy will build up a tolerance to this and may be more likely to charge at the player, forcing them to change their tactics. This change in the enemy's behaviour keeps the player more engaged and focused on the encounter due to the increased challenge of having to adapt their tactics mid battle to be successful.

Finally, 'The Four Treasures' also implements dynamic difficulty adjustment to cater to a wider audience. Players initially select a static difficulty of “easy”, “regular” or “hard”. This selection sets an initial multiplier in the game that changes variables such as the enemies health, speed and player strength. The player's progress is then monitored in each level to see if they are struggling or progressing easily. Depending on this, the initial difficulty multipliers are adjusted. These seemingly minor changes enable the player to stay in a state of flow reducing the risk of them becoming bored or frustrated.
